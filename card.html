The Elephant

    Elephants are the largest animals in the world and are well recognised by their long trunks.

  • Fact 1: Largest animals in the world
  • Fact 2: Can live to be over 70 years old
    • Fact 3: Only one mammal can't jump - the elephant
    Fact 4: An elephant's skin is an inch thick

Elephants are the largest land mammals on Earth. Two species of elephant, the Asian elephant and African elephant, remain extant. Elephants are herbivores and prefer habitats that give them access to food and water. Both species consume as many as 300 pounds of food per day.
